# AK(3) Neuwirth theory design

Date: 2026-07-23

Status: design approved by the user's theory-first directive; all proposed mathematical statements remain unverified until the hostile review and proof checks pass.

## Scope and result bar

This subproject extracts a rigorous thickenability criterion for the presentation 2-complexes of AK(3) and its orbit-2 representative directly from Neuwirth's 1968 permutation formalism. It then applies a second, dependency-free implementation of that formalism to both presentations.

The target presentations are:

- AK(3): `xxxYYYY | xyxYXY`;
- orbit-2: `YYXXyx | YYYxyXX`.

The repository's 17-move certificate already proves that orbit-2 is classically AC-equivalent to AK(3). AK(3)'s stable AC-triviality remains open; stale statements to the contrary are not premises of this work.

The exact deliverables are:

1. a theorem and proof giving a necessary-and-sufficient finite Neuwirth criterion for thickenability when the Whitehead/link graph is connected;
2. an explicit dictionary between relator occurrences and Neuwirth's permutations \(A,B,C\), including the orientation-reversing coupling at the two ends of each generator;
3. an independent exhaustive decision certificate for the two target complexes over all \(5!\,6!=86{,}400\) compatible cyclic-order choices each;
4. an adversarial report that states precisely what the decisions do and do not imply.

A positive thickenability verdict would count only after an independent Regina construction and `isBall()` check, as required by the operating contract. A negative verdict for either base complex says only that this particular presentation complex is not thickenable. It does not prove that AK(3) is an AC or stable-AC counterexample.

## Primary-source foundation

The load-bearing source is L. Neuwirth, "An algorithm for the construction of 3-manifolds from 2-complexes," Proc. Cambridge Philos. Soc. 64 (1968), 603-614.

For a one-vertex presentation complex, Neuwirth assigns:

- a set \(E\) with two elements for every letter occurrence;
- a fixed-point-free involution \(A\) pairing the endpoints of corners between consecutive letters of relators;
- a fixed-point-free involution \(B\) pairing the two ends of each occurrence through its generator tube;
- a permutation \(C\) encoding a cyclic order of occurrences around each generator end.

If the cyclic order at a positive generator end is
\((e^1_i,e^2_i,\ldots,e^{r(i)}_i)\), the paired negative-end cycle in Neuwirth is
\((\bar e^{r(i)}_i,\ldots,\bar e^2_i,\bar e^1_i)\). This is the reversal convention that the existing prototype labels unverified.

Let \(L\) be the number of orbits of the group generated by \(A\) and \(C\). Neuwirth's Euler condition is

\[
|A|-|C|+2L=|AC|,
\]

where \(|X|\) denotes the number of cycles of a permutation \(X\). For the connected-link case \(L=1\), this is exactly the genus-zero condition for the induced rotation surface.

## Proposed theorem

The proposed statement is deliberately limited to the connected-link regime needed for AK(3) and orbit-2.

> Let \(K_P\) be the canonical one-vertex 2-complex of a finite presentation \(P\), with every 1-cell incident to a 2-cell and conversely. Assume its Whitehead/link graph is connected. Then \(K_P\) is thickenable in an orientable 3-manifold if and only if there is a cyclic ordering of the occurrences of each generator for which Neuwirth's associated permutations satisfy
> \[
> |A|-|C|+2=|AC|.
> \]
> The second transitivity condition in Neuwirth's Theorem 2 is not part of thickenability: it is the additional condition that the boundary of the constructed regular neighbourhood is connected and hence, in Neuwirth's setting, a 2-sphere that can be capped by one 3-cell.

For a balanced presentation of the trivial group, any thickening \(N(K_P)\) deformation-retracts onto a contractible complex, so \(N(K_P)\) is a compact contractible orientable 3-manifold and therefore a 3-ball. In this special case the boundary-connectivity conclusion follows automatically after thickenability has been established. Lackenby's Theorem 1.3 then makes a positive verdict an unstable AC-triviality certificate.

## Proof architecture

The proof is split into four independently reviewable lemmas.

### Lemma 1: occurrence-permutation dictionary

Construct \(E,A,B\) canonically from the cyclic relator words and show that a choice of one cyclic order per unsigned generator determines \(C\) with the reversed paired order at the opposite end. Prove that this is bijective, modulo rotation of each chosen cyclic order, with the edge-compatible rotation systems used by the link-graph formulation.

### Lemma 2: Euler equivalence

Show that the link surface defined by the rotation system has:

- vertices counted by \(|C|\);
- edges counted by \(|A|\);
- faces counted by \(|AC|\).

For a connected link graph, the surface has genus zero exactly when
\(|A|-|C|+2=|AC|\). This also proves that the prototype's face tracing and Neuwirth's cycle equation are the same computation, not merely analogous tests.

### Lemma 3: necessity

If \(K_P\) embeds in an orientable 3-manifold, intersect it with a small oriented 2-sphere around the unique vertex. The resulting embedded link graph supplies compatible cyclic orders, and its sphere embedding forces the Euler equation.

### Lemma 4: sufficiency and the role of transitivity

Follow the constructive part of Neuwirth's proof of Theorem 2 only through the Euler step. A genus-zero compatible ordering embeds the link graph on the boundary of a 0-handle; the reversed pairing extends the incident bands through orientable 1-handles; the remaining 2-cell discs attach and thicken to a compact orientable 3-manifold containing \(K_P\) as a spine. Neuwirth's additional transitivity hypothesis is invoked only afterward to make the complement of the attaching curves connected, equivalently to obtain one spherical boundary component suitable for a single 3-cell.

For a balanced trivial-group presentation, contractibility of the resulting regular neighbourhood forces its boundary to be a 2-sphere; capping and the 3-dimensional Poincare theorem identify the neighbourhood with a 3-ball.

## Independent finite certificate

The certificate implementation will be a new, small Python module that does not import `check_thickenable.py` and does not reuse its dart or genus routines.

For each target it will:

1. parse the two cyclic relators into occurrence IDs;
2. build the permutations \(A\) and \(B\) directly from Neuwirth's definitions;
3. enumerate cyclic orders at the positive ends of `x` and `y`, fixing one occurrence in each order to quotient cyclic rotation;
4. construct the negative cycles by reversed \(B\)-pairing;
5. compute \(C\), the number of \(\langle A,C\rangle\)-orbits, and \(|AC|\);
6. record a histogram of Euler defects and any accepting order;
7. hash a canonical serialization of the complete enumeration summary.

AK(3) and orbit-2 both have generator degrees 6 and 7 and connected link graphs, so each exhaustive run has exactly \(5!\,6!=86{,}400\) cases. The certificate must assert that the enumerated count is exactly 86,400; an early-exit-only negative is not accepted.

The existing prototype will be run only as a comparison oracle. Agreement between the two implementations is evidence; the proof and the independent permutation census are the certificate.

## Verification

The verification gates are:

1. visually inspect the primary Neuwirth pages containing the definition of \(C\) and Theorems 1-2;
2. check the occurrence dictionary by hand on Neuwirth's worked presentation \(\langle a_1,a_2\mid a_1^3,[a_1,a_2]\rangle\);
3. reproduce the standard, commutator, trefoil-spine, and \(K_{3,3}\)-link calibration cases without importing the prototype;
4. compare the verdict and accepting-order count with the existing implementation on the two targets; retain the new implementation's Euler-defect histogram as its own completeness audit;
5. have a hostile reviewer try to refute the theorem, especially the sufficiency step and the omission of Neuwirth's transitivity condition;
6. mark any positive on an open target as a suspected bug until a separately constructed Regina triangulation returns `isBall() == True`.

No search above `node_budget = 1000` is involved. The finite rotation enumeration is a decision procedure, not an AC search.

## Alternatives not selected

### One-stabilization normal form

Classifying arbitrary rank-2 to rank-3 to rank-2 stable derivations could produce a stronger search theorem, but the normalization must control arbitrary conjugators and unbounded van Kampen area. That is too close to the open stable problem for a first deliverable.

### Exact multi-substitution orbit escape

An algebraic characterization of which \(k\ge2\) CoVs leave an \(\operatorname{Aut}(F_2)\)-orbit would be useful, but the existing theorem program already contains the orbit-escape threshold, exact length laws, and counterexamples to floor invariance. Its immediate leverage on AK(3) is lower.

### Production greedy from orbit-2

This remains a valid empirical lead, but it is implementation-heavy and cannot be run at a decisive budget locally. It is deferred in response to the user's theory-first direction.
