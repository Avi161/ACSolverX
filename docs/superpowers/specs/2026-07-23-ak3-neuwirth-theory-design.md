# AK(3) Neuwirth theory design

Date: 2026-07-23

Status: the base theorem and two independent 86,400-case target replays are complete. The component phase was redesigned after its preflight exposed factorial costs up to 958,003,200 order pairs. The replacement is an exact signed-rank planarity criterion, not a larger brute-force run.

## Scope and result bar

This subproject extracts a rigorous thickenability criterion for the presentation 2-complexes of AK(3) and its orbit-2 representative directly from Neuwirth's 1968 permutation formalism. It then applies a second, dependency-free implementation of that formalism to both presentations.

The target presentations are:

- AK(3): `xxxYYYY | xyxYXY`;
- orbit-2: `YYXXyx | YYYxyXX`.

The repository's 17-edge Definition-2.1 macro certificate already proves that orbit-2 is classically AC-equivalent to AK(3). This is not a claim of 17 elementary AC1--AC3 moves. AK(3)'s stable AC-triviality remains open; stale statements to the contrary are not premises of this work.

The exact deliverables are:

1. a theorem and proof giving a necessary-and-sufficient finite Neuwirth criterion for thickenability of the balanced presentation complexes needed here when the Whitehead/link graph is connected;
2. an explicit dictionary between relator occurrences and Neuwirth's permutations \(A,B,C\), including the orientation-reversing coupling at the two ends of each generator;
3. an independent exhaustive decision certificate for the two target complexes over all \(5!\,6!=86{,}400\) compatible cyclic-order choices each;
4. the exact Neuwirth genus potential
   \[
   \gamma_N(P)=\min_C\frac{|A|-|C|+2L-|AC|}{2},
   \]
   which vanishes exactly at thickenable presentation complexes but is intentionally not claimed to be AC-invariant;
5. an adversarial report that states precisely what the decisions do and do not imply.

A positive thickenability verdict would count only after an independent Regina construction and `isBall()` check, as required by the operating contract. A negative verdict for either base complex says only that this particular presentation complex is not thickenable. It does not prove that AK(3) is an AC or stable-AC counterexample.

## Primary-source foundation

The load-bearing source is L. Neuwirth, "An algorithm for the construction of 3-manifolds from 2-complexes," Proc. Cambridge Philos. Soc. 64 (1968), 603-614.

For a one-vertex presentation complex, Neuwirth assigns:

- a set \(E\) with two elements for every letter occurrence;
- a fixed-point-free involution \(A\) pairing the endpoints of corners between consecutive letters of relators;
- a fixed-point-free involution \(B\) pairing the two ends of each occurrence through its generator tube;
- a permutation \(C\) encoding a cyclic order of occurrences around each generator end.

If the cyclic order at a positive generator end is
\((e^1_i,e^2_i,\ldots,e^{r(i)}_i)\), the paired negative-end cycle in Neuwirth is
\((\bar e^{r(i)}_i,\ldots,\bar e^2_i,\bar e^1_i)\). This is the reversal convention that the existing prototype labels unverified.

Let \(L\) be the number of orbits of the group generated by \(A\) and \(C\). Neuwirth's Euler condition is

\[
|A|-|C|+2L=|AC|,
\]

where \(|X|\) denotes the number of cycles of a permutation \(X\). For the connected-link case \(L=1\), this is exactly the genus-zero condition for the induced rotation surface.

Permutation products act right-to-left throughout: \(AC(e)=A(C(e))\). Replacing \(AC\) by \(CA\) does not change its cycle count, since the two products are conjugate. Transitivity products are not interchangeable: Neuwirth's Theorem 1 prints \(\langle AC,CB\rangle\), whereas Theorem 2 and the geometric proof on pp. 610--611 use \(\langle AC,BC\rangle\). The proof's \(\langle AC,BC\rangle\) convention is the one audited here, and the conflicting printed formula is recorded rather than silently normalized.

## Proposed theorem

The proposed statement is deliberately limited to the connected-link regime needed for AK(3) and orbit-2.

> Let \(K_P\) be the canonical one-vertex 2-complex of a finite balanced presentation \(P\), with every 1-cell incident to a 2-cell and conversely. Assume its Whitehead/link graph is connected. Then \(K_P\) is thickenable in an orientable 3-manifold if and only if there is a cyclic ordering of the occurrences of each generator for which Neuwirth's associated permutations satisfy
> \[
> |A|-|C|+2=|AC|.
> \]
> The second transitivity condition in Neuwirth's Theorem 2 is not part of thickenability: it is the additional condition that the boundary of the constructed regular neighbourhood is connected and hence, in Neuwirth's balanced setting, a 2-sphere that can be capped by one 3-cell.

This is an extraction of the construction in Neuwirth's proof, not a claim that his stated Theorem 2 omits transitivity. Neuwirth's direct presentation-complex construction is load-bearing. The modern compatible-spherical-link theorem quoted by Fulek--Tóth is corroboration only: its loopless multigraph/cyclic-facet formulation is not applied to a one-vertex presentation complex without first proving a homeomorphism-preserving PL subdivision bridge.

For a balanced presentation of the trivial group, Euler characteristic and trivial \(\pi_1\) make \(K_P\) acyclic and simply connected, hence contractible. Any regular neighbourhood \(N(K_P)\) retracts onto \(K_P\). Poincare--Lefschetz duality makes \(\partial N\) connected with the homology of \(S^2\), so surface classification gives \(\partial N\cong S^2\). Capping by a ball, van Kampen, the 3-dimensional Poincare theorem, and PL Schoenflies give \(N\cong B^3\). Thus the correct boundary transitivity condition is automatic for a balanced trivial-group positive. Lackenby's Theorem 1.3 then makes a positive verdict an unstable AC-triviality certificate.

## Proof architecture

The proof is split into four independently reviewable lemmas.

### Lemma 1: occurrence-permutation dictionary

Construct \(E,A,B\) canonically from the exact cyclic relator words and show that a choice of one cyclic order per unsigned generator determines \(C\) with the reversed paired order at the opposite end. Prove that this is bijective, modulo rotation of each chosen cyclic order, with the compatible local orderings in Neuwirth's construction. Do not freely or cyclically reduce input words: the claim concerns the original word-realized CW complex, and thickenability is not being used as a homotopy invariant.

### Lemma 2: Euler equivalence

Show that the link surface defined by the rotation system has:

- vertices counted by \(|C|\);
- edges counted by \(|A|\);
- faces counted by \(|AC|\).

For a connected link graph, the surface has genus zero exactly when
\(|A|-|C|+2=|AC|\). This also proves that the prototype's face tracing and Neuwirth's cycle equation are the same computation, not merely analogous tests.

### Lemma 3: necessity

If \(K_P\) embeds in an orientable 3-manifold, intersect it with a small oriented 2-sphere around the unique vertex. The resulting embedded link graph supplies compatible cyclic orders, and its sphere embedding forces the Euler equation.

### Lemma 4: sufficiency, regular neighbourhoods, and the role of transitivity

Follow the constructive part of Neuwirth's proof of Theorem 2 only through the Euler step, making the regular-neighbourhood claim explicit. A genus-zero compatible ordering embeds the exact occurrence link on the boundary of a 0-handle. The reversed \(B\)-pairing attaches orientable 1-handles with product neighbourhoods of every incident band. Attaching a product \(D^2\times[-1,1]\) along the two-sided annular neighbourhood of each remaining attaching curve gives a compact orientable PL 3-manifold \(N\). Its 0-, 1-, and 2-handle cores are exactly the cells and attaching maps of \(K_P\); radial collapse on each product handle is compatible on overlaps, so \(N\searrow K_P\). Thus \(N\) is a regular neighbourhood of the exact presentation complex, not merely an ambient manifold containing a homotopy-equivalent complex. Neuwirth's additional \(\langle AC,BC\rangle\)-transitivity hypothesis is invoked only afterward to make the complement of the attaching curves connected, equivalently to obtain one spherical boundary component suitable for a single 3-cell.

For a balanced trivial-group presentation, the duality/Poincare/Schoenflies argument above makes the resulting regular neighbourhood a 3-ball. Consequently, an Euler-accepting order that fails the correctly implemented \(\langle AC,BC\rangle\) boundary test is an implementation-or-dictionary contradiction, not a new topological case.

## Independent finite certificate

The certificate implementation will be a new, small Python module that does not import `check_thickenable.py` and does not reuse its dart or genus routines.

For each target it will:

1. parse the two cyclic relators into occurrence IDs;
2. build the permutations \(A\) and \(B\) directly from Neuwirth's definitions;
3. enumerate cyclic orders at the positive ends of `x` and `y`, fixing one occurrence in each order to quotient cyclic rotation;
4. construct the negative cycles by reversed \(B\)-pairing;
5. compute \(C\), the number of \(\langle A,C\rangle\)-orbits, and \(|AC|\);
6. compute \(\langle AC,BC\rangle\)-transitivity for every order satisfying the Euler equation;
7. record a histogram of Euler defects, all accepting orders, and their transitivity status;
8. hash the ordered per-case trace, not merely the aggregate summary.

AK(3) and orbit-2 both have generator degrees 6 and 7 and connected link graphs, so each exhaustive run has exactly \(5!\,6!=86{,}400\) cases. The certificate must assert that the enumerated count is exactly 86,400; an early-exit-only negative is not accepted.

The implementation fails closed. No Euler-accepting order gives `NOT_THICKENABLE` by necessity. An Euler-accepting, transitive order gives `THICKENABLE_NEUWIRTH` directly by Neuwirth's stated theorem. On a target independently known to be a balanced trivial-group presentation, an Euler-accepting but nontransitive order gives `AUDIT_CONTRADICTION`: Lemma 4 and contractibility predict thickenability and connected boundary, so the result is quarantined as a proof/dictionary/code inconsistency rather than called either a positive or a theory gap.

The existing prototype will be run only as a comparison oracle. A second new dart/rotation enumerator will emit the same canonical per-case trace independently of the Neuwirth-permutation implementation. The target strings, right-to-left composition convention, source commit, exact case count, implementation hashes, trace digests, defect histogram, and accepting orders form the replay identity. Agreement between both implementations is evidence; the proof and the replayable finite census are the certificate.

## Verification

The verification gates are:

1. visually inspect the primary Neuwirth pages containing the definition of \(C\) and Theorems 1-2;
2. check the occurrence dictionary by hand on Neuwirth's worked presentation \(\langle a_1,a_2\mid a_1^3,[a_1,a_2]\rangle\);
3. reproduce the standard, commutator, trefoil-spine, and \(K_{3,3}\)-link calibration cases without importing the prototype; the exact balanced presentation \(\langle x,y\mid x,xyXY\rangle\) supplies a connected-link order that satisfies Euler but fails the correct \(\langle AC,BC\rangle\)-transitivity test;
4. pin the exact word \(xxX\) with the occurrence data from the hostile review: it passes Euler and correct \(\langle AC,BC\rangle\)-transitivity, while the printed \(\langle AC,CB\rangle\) expression has two orbits;
5. compare the exact ordered per-case digest, verdict, and accepting-order count between the two independent new implementations on the targets; retain the Euler-defect histogram as a human-auditable completeness check;
6. have a hostile reviewer try to refute the theorem, especially the sufficiency step and the omission of Neuwirth's transitivity condition;
7. mark any positive on an open target as a suspected bug until a separately constructed Regina triangulation returns `isBall() == True` and its handle-incidence map is checked against the exact accepted occurrence ordering.

No search above `node_budget = 1000` is involved. The finite rotation enumeration is a decision procedure, not an AC search.

## Alternatives not selected

### One-stabilization normal form

Classifying arbitrary rank-2 to rank-3 to rank-2 stable derivations could produce a stronger search theorem, but the normalization must control arbitrary conjugators and unbounded van Kampen area. That is too close to the open stable problem for a first deliverable.

### Exact multi-substitution orbit escape

An algebraic characterization of which \(k\ge2\) CoVs leave an \(\operatorname{Aut}(F_2)\)-orbit would be useful, but the existing theorem program already contains the orbit-escape threshold, exact length laws, and counterexamples to floor invariance. Its immediate leverage on AK(3) is lower.

### Production greedy from orbit-2

This remains a valid empirical lead, but it is implementation-heavy and cannot be run at a decisive budget locally. It is deferred in response to the user's theory-first direction.

## Second revision: eliminate the factorial component scan

The old component plan would decide only the 18 of 1,000 height-17 states whose
raw cyclic-order cost is at most 2,000,000.  The support census is much more
structured:

- 720 states have simple support \(K_4\);
- 278 have support \(K_4-e\), split equally between the two missing
  same-generator edges;
- 2 have support \(C_4\);
- none has a link loop or any other support.

This permits an exact theory-first replacement.

### Spherical rotation theorem for \(K_4\) multigraphs

For a loopless multigraph with simple support \(K_4\), a rotation system is
spherical exactly when every parallel class is one cyclic interval at both
endpoints, the six contracted intervals form one of the two mirror tetrahedral
rotations, and the labeled edge orders at opposite ends of every class are
reverses.  Necessity follows by deleting the two endpoints of a class: the
remaining graph is connected, so all nonclass edges occupy one angular gap.
Deleting all but one edge per class leaves the unique spherical embedding of
\(K_4\).  Sufficiency follows by replacing the six edges of that embedding by
parallel ribbons.

After fixing one mirror orientation, give edge \(e\) in class \(P_{uv}\) a rank
\(z_e\in\{0,\ldots,m_{uv}-1\}\), all different within the class.  The two
endpoint slots are \(z_e\) and \(m_{uv}-1-z_e\), offset by their block starts.
Unknown cyclic origins at the paired generator ends contribute only two phases
\(S_x,S_y\).  Neuwirth's reversal condition becomes one modular equation for
each \(B\)-pair:

\[
P_d(z_{e(d)})+P_{Bd}(z_{e(Bd)})+S_g\equiv0\pmod{n_g}.
\]

Contracting the \(A\)-edges leaves a degree-two constraint graph, namely the
relator cycles.  For fixed phases, one seed rank determines a whole cycle.
An individual cycle assignment is admissible only if it never repeats a rank
inside any parallel class.  Two relator assignments combine only if their
six class-rank bitsets are pairwise disjoint **and**, for every class
\(P_{uv}\), their union has cardinality \(m_{uv}\).  The last assertion is
mathematically redundant once every one of the \(m_{uv}\) edge variables has
been assigned injectively, but it is an explicit certificate invariant rather
than an inference hidden in code.  This replaces the factorial enumeration by
a finite phase/seed calculation with an independently reconstructed face
trace.

### The two proper supports

For \(K_4-cd\), let \(a,b\) be the other vertices.  The central parallel class
\(P_{ab}\) need not be one block.  The exact spherical schemes are

\[
C,\ L[0:i],\ D,\ L[i:m]\qquad(0\le i\le m),
\]

where \(C,D\) are the two branch blocks through \(c,d\), and \(L\) is the
rank-ordered list of direct \(ab\)-edges.  The order at \(b\) is reversed; the
four leg classes remain reversed endpoint blocks.  Thus only \(m+1\) schemes
are needed.  For \(C_4\), every parallel class is a reversed endpoint block and
there is one scheme.

These support classifications and the signed-rank equivalence are
load-bearing and must be proved before implementation.  General loopless
supports are an instance of Synchronized Planarity; the specialized proof is
kept because it is smaller, auditable, and exactly covers the component.

### Certificate bar for the full component

The rank solver must fail closed on an unclassified support.  It must be
cross-checked against the factorial enumerator on all 18 affordable component
states and on the exhaustive deterministic set of small exact two-relator
systems specified in the implementation plan.  A reported positive must
include reconstructed rotations and an independent genus-zero face trace,
then remain `REGINA_REQUIRED`.

The component result is replayable rather than a one-way summary.  Its source
identity includes the root words, height ceiling, move generator and
canonicalization implementations, every move option, cap semantics, exact
sorted state-set digest, actual pop count, queue exhaustion, and an explicit
closure check.  Verification rebuilds the component and reruns the complete
support/scheme/phase/seed decision for every state, including every negative.
A negative is a thickenability decision for the exact word-realized complex
only; it is not an AC invariant.  The run remains exactly the existing
1,000-state height-17 computation and is not an enlarged AC search.
