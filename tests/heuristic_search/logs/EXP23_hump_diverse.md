# EXP-23 — the second hump, attacked from the other family

Bins 8–9 plus the six reach rows (18 presentations), budget 1000, cap 64. The orderings here are deliberately **not** knot climbs: they are the families that complemented the recommended ordering on the decidable band, plus two led by second-family features that have never been pointed at this tier.

Every previous attempt on this tier (EXP-08, EXP-12) used one family. Since the finalists turn out to be redundant with each other — at budget 1,000 the union of all five equals the best single — 'nothing solves the second hump' would have been a claim about that family alone.

**Nothing solved — now across both families.** The expected result: these presentations survived a 10^6-node search and 1000 nodes is three orders of magnitude short, which no reordering closes on its own. What this adds to EXP-08 and EXP-12 is that the negative is no longer specific to knot climbs.
