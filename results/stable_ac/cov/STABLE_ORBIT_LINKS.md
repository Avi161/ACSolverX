# Stable orbit links across the CoV sweeps (aca124 @ 50k + s60r6 @ 10k)

Generated by `experiments/stable_ac/cov/orbit_links.py` from `covsweep_50000_124_subnc2pxysb_mrl24_cyc_aca124_07_21_26.jsonl` (11,370 rows: 124 controls + 11,246 CoV starts, **0 solved**) and `covsweep_10000_66_subnc2pxysb_mrl24_cyc_s60r6_07_20_26.jsonl`.

**Method.** Every CoV row is a stable-AC equivalence between its input's Aut(F₂)-orbit (`aut_canon_orig`) and its output's orbit (`aut_canon_cov`): the transform is stabilize → substitute → isolate → destabilize, and Aut-equivalence implies stable equivalence (the stable ambient principle — the only proved form; the unstable version is open). Union-find over orbit canonical strings therefore groups orbits that are provably **stably** AC-equivalent, using only fields the sweeps already computed. Trivializable seeds: the subset tier (rows of `ms640_solved.txt`) and the output orbit of every solved CoV row.

## Findings

1. **No stable merges among the 124 unsolved classes.** Their 124 input orbits stay pairwise distinct (confirming the Aut-quotient) and land in 124 separate components; none of the 919 distinct orbit-moving CoV outputs of the aca sweep collides with another aca class or with any trivializable orbit. The 50k sweep therefore certifies no new solvability, stable or otherwise — consistent with its 0/11,370 solve count.
2. **The reach tier adds zero problems beyond the 124.** All six reach presentations sit in the *same input orbit* as an aca class member: `25_17 ∈ aca_107`, `AK(3) ∈ aca_115`, `14_1 ∈ aca_116`, `17_41 ∈ aca_2`, `19_40 ∈ aca_5`, `25_1 ∈ aca_99`. For the five MS-named ones this is by construction of the classes; for AK(3) it confirms the AK/MS-family identification inside our own data: **AK(3) is a plain Aut(F₂)-relabel of class aca_115's representative**, so the aca124 sweep's aca_115 family (and its 50k failure) is an AK(3) result, and any future solve of aca_115 is a solve of AK(3).
3. **The method has real detection power — shown on the solved tier.** Among the 60 subset presentations, one-shot CoV edges merge 15 presentations into one stable component (`43, 48, 77, 141, 155, 201, 203, 217, 247, 265, 288, 327, 333, 380, 533`), plus five smaller CoV-linked clusters; separately, the 8 presentations greedy never solved at 10k (`622–625, 636–639`) are all one input orbit — the benchmark's eight "hardest" subset rows are a single problem in eight disguises. So the null result on the 124 is informative, not a blind spot: one-shot subword CoVs do collide across presentations when the underlying problems are close.

**Ledger.** Open classes remain **124**; the 66-benchmark's 6 never-solved reach problems are members of 6 of them. Next escalations that could move the number: iterated CoV (compose two transforms — the orbit reach grows quadratically), or seeding the ACA merge search from the 919 fresh moved orbits this sweep discovered per class.
