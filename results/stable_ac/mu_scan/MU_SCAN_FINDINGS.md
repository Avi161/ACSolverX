## Findings

1. **19 of the 124 classes have a strictly descending orbit-floor path within two gated subword-CoV hops — 15 of them hop-2-ONLY.** The hop-2-only descents climb first, then drop below the input floor: aca_97/98 go μ 24 → 27 (hop 1) → **19** (hop 2); aca_81/85: 22 → **18**; aca_58/123: 20 → **17**; aca_34/122: 18 → **16**; aca_120/121: 16 → **15**; aca_72: 20 → 18; aca_95: 21 → 19; aca_43/44: 19 → 18; aca_88: 23 → 21; plus the four hop-1 descenders (aca_99/100: 25 → 22, aca_105/106: 25 → 24). This is the uphill-then-downhill shape the floor refutation predicted — and it is exactly what a per-hop-μ-pruning tree would have missed.
2. **The 50k sweep never searched any of these.** It searched hop-1 outputs only; the descended hop-2 orbits are virgin territory for the greedy. The 28 concrete descended start pairs (every chain re-derived and orbit-μ-verified) are exported as **`data/ms_unsolved_reps/mu_descents_d2.csv`** (`name,r1,r2,source_class,mu_source,mu,hops,z_path`), loadable as bench `mu_descents_d2` in the portfolio runner.
3. **Certificate accounting**: each exported start is reached from its class rep by 1–2 CoV hops (stable supermoves), so a greedy solve from it certifies **stable AC-triviality** of the whole source class — segmented certificate `[CoV chain] + [greedy path]`, same convention as the restart tree. A solve here would remove one of the 124 from the stable ledger.
4. Reach growth: 919 distinct orbits at hop 1 → **4,772** cumulative at hop 2 (~5× per hop, no saturation), consistent with the shipped geometric-growth remark.
5. Scope: `keep = 10` lowest-μ hop-1 orbits expanded per class, cap 24 — a wider `keep` can only ADD descents; these 19 are a lower bound.
6. **Cap-48 arm: exact null.** The full cap-48 re-scan (`mu_scan_aca124_d2_k10_mrl48.jsonl`) reproduces the cap-24 map identically — same 19 descending classes, same best floors, same 919/4,772 orbit reach. At these input lengths the 24-cap rejects no depth-2 CoV candidate that matters; the high-cap hypothesis (user's T9 push) is not falsified but its payoff, if any, lives deeper in the iterated tree or inside the 50k searches (watch `max_popped_total` in the stall-escape rows), not at tree depth 2.

## What the 90× buys: essentially nothing, and that is the result

**Every floor was first touched by rung 9.** Across all 36 descenders the final `best_mu` first appears at rung 2 (14 classes), 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, or — once, `aca_100` — rung 9. **98% of the rungs that ran, ran after their class's last gain.** The 256-rung ladder is a ~10-rung ladder with 340 CPU-hours of tail. Against the closed prior census (35/124, r8 beam 12 + beam 24) this bought **one new descender, `aca_67` (21 → 20)**, plus 4 further classes with deeper floors — `aca_87` 21→20, `aca_100` 22→20, `aca_106` 23→21, `aca_109` 24→23 — for a census of **36/124 descending, 88 undescended**. Zero regressions: the beam-64 ladder dominates both prior ladders on every class, so the floors are stable under a 5× beam widening even where they did not improve.

**The front drifts up, hard, and now it is measured.** Of 5,579,015 orbits only **367 (0.0066%) sit below their own class's starting μ** — the median descending class has 4.5 orbits below its start out of ~44,000 explored, `aca_99` the most at 73. 97.5% of all orbits are above μ 24; the distribution runs to **μ 302**. This is the `rung-local-beam-abandons-the-low-shell` lesson at 90× scale: a rung-local beam spends effectively all of its compute climbing the cone, and buying more rungs buys more of the wrong shell. The operational conclusion is not "search deeper" but "the beam ladder is exhausted as a paradigm" — a global best-first heap over the open set is the only version of this that can make a closure claim, and it is what the next arm should be.

## Three collision scans over the full 5.58M — all null, one-sided

The orbit dump makes cross-checks possible that the previous runs (35 floor reps) could not attempt. Seed orbits were canonicalized with the pure-Python `aut_canon` and every one of the 113 is a fixed point of the numba twin the dump was keyed with, so these are real nulls, not key mismatches; the detector was also confirmed to fire on a planted duplicate.

1. **ms640: 0 hits.** No orbit of any class, at any rung, lands in any of the 113 distinct Aut-orbits of the 640 solved presentations. Previously tested on 35 floor reps; now on 5.58M × 113 ≈ 630M pairings. A hit would have certified the source class stably AC-trivial.
2. **Cross-class: 0 shared orbits.** All 5,579,015 orbits are globally distinct — no two of the 124 classes meet anywhere in their ladders. A shared orbit would have been a stable-AC merge in the 124-class ledger; there are none within reach of this move set at this depth.
3. **AK(3)'s orbit: reached only by `aca_115`, at rung 0** — i.e. its own starting orbit, which was already known. No other class collapses onto AK(3).

Every null here is **one-sided**: a hit is valid (the orbit really was reached), a miss proves nothing. 48 of the 124 rows were cut by the 4h wall-clock backstop rather than run to their deterministic budget (none hit the 150k orbit cap; min rungs completed 38), so those orbit sets are incomplete *and* machine-dependent. Because every floor landed by rung 9, the timeouts cost no floor — they bound only the (already null) exhaustive claims.

## The MS template holds at beam 64

The two-hop template `z₁ = xy, z₂ = xᵏ` reappears untouched on exactly the same 10 classes, one pair per k: k=3 `aca_120/121` (16→15), k=4 `aca_34/122` (18→16), k=5 `aca_58/123` (20→17), k=6 `aca_81/85` (22→18), k=7 `aca_97/98` (24→19). So widening the beam 5× and deepening 32× found nothing that beats the template on its own family. The non-template descenders' chains are long and idiosyncratic (up to 9 hops, e.g. `aca_100`), with no second pattern repeating across classes.

## What this closes and what it opens

Closed: rung depth. Nothing in the r8 → r256 range is unexploited, and a further deepening of a rung-local beam is not worth the compute. Open, in priority order: (1) the **global best-first heap** over the open set — the only route to a closure claim rather than a beam report, already prototyped on `aca_39`; (2) the 88 undescended classes are still **not** evidence of a wall (beam + cap 24 + 48 timeouts); (3) the ms640 null argues, as before, that the payoff is in *enlarging the trivializable seed library* (113 orbits is the real surface, not 640 rows), not in more ladder.
