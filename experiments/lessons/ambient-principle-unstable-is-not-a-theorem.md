# [2026-07-17] The pairwise "apply an automorphism" AC-principle is only sound stably, or in chains ending at the standard tuple [TRAP]

`literature/proofs/BACKGROUND.tex` stated as classical: if `⟨⟨r1,r2⟩⟩ = F₂` and `φ ∈ Aut(F₂)` then `(r1,r2) ~AC (φr1, φr2)` — the **unstable** pairwise ambient automorphism principle, implicitly sourced to mms02. Checking the sources shows that claim is not a theorem:

- **mms02** (arXiv:math/0302080, §"Applying an automorphism") asserts it with **no proof** (`⟨Y⟩_F` there is the normal closure — verified in `literature/txt/mms02_andrews_curtis_equivalence.txt`). The paper's own AC-trivializations only use automorphism steps inside chains that terminate at `⟨x,y | x,y⟩`, where the collapse lemma of Burns–Macedońska / Miasnikov makes them removable — so mms02's *usage* is sound even though the blanket pairwise statement is not established.
- **Panteleev–Ushakov 2016** (arXiv:1609.00325, read from the PDF): prove `φ(AK(n)) ~AC AK(n)` for all `φ` (Prop 3.7) by computer-found chains for a generating set of `Aut(F₂)`; state it is unknown whether automorphism moves are subsumed by AC moves "(even for presentations of 1)"; and **conjecture** the pairwise principle *fails* for some balanced presentation of 1 (which would refute ACC). A 2016 paper by the same school treating it as open is decisive against mms02's 2003 assertion.
- The sound general form is **stable**: `(r1,r2) ~st (φr1, φr2)` for every balanced presentation of the trivial group and every `φ` — proved in `literature/proofs/PROOFS.tex` (Theorem "Stable ambient automorphism principle", 2026-07-17) via Lemma 11 of arXiv:2408.15332: two generator exchanges per elementary map `x ↦ y^p x^± y^q`, AC3 for inner. Sharp: the normal closure `⟨⟨r1,r2⟩⟩ ⊆ F₂` is a stable invariant (also proved there), so the triviality hypothesis is necessary.

**Rule:** never cite the unstable pairwise automorphism principle. Cite (a) the stable form (`PROOFS.tex`), or (b) the end-at-standard collapse (P–U Lemma 3.1) when a derivation reaches `(x,y)`. More generally: before citing a "classical" principle, pin down its exact strength — stable vs unstable, generation vs normal generation — against the source text, not a summary. `BACKGROUND.tex` was fixed 2026-07-17 to state the stable form.
