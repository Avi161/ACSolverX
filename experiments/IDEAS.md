# IDEAS.md — stable-AC search: ranked research directions

Working idea board for the project's core bet: use **stable Andrews–Curtis moves (AC1–AC5)** — above all the Lemma-11 supermove that adjoins a generator `z = w(x,y)` at an *unknown-but-finite* elementary-move cost — to lift the search **out of length local-minima** and solve difficult balanced presentations of the trivial group. The concrete targets are the **124 distinct unsolved Miller–Schupp problems** (the 261 unsolved representatives collapse to 124 classes; see `experiments/equivalence_classes/`) and, as the field's marquee open case, **AK(3)** (stable-triviality **OPEN** — never treated as settled here). The companion theorem program lives in `literature/proofs/STABLE_AC_NEW.tex`; ranked engineering ideas live here.

## TL;DR — read this before acting

**What this is:** 14 viable + 9 dead research directions (ranked highest→lowest potential, each vetted by a domain-expert referee), 7 proven/checked theorems about the search machinery, and one runnable planning tool.

**What will *not* work — the load-bearing caveat.** None of the CoV-based levers here is expected to *solve* a hard case by itself. The strongest lever found — abelianized-magnitude ranking of CoV candidates (idea 1), and the `restart_planner.py` tool built on it — is a **solution-depth proxy**: strong on shallow / already-solvable instances, and provably **weakest exactly on the hard residual and AK(3)** it will be pointed at (those have near-identity abelianization yet astronomical AC-distance). Iterated CoV reaches an order of magnitude more restart orbits (AK(3): 2→12→55) but **never lowers AK(3)'s length-13 floor** — more restart points, none closer to trivial; reachability is *not* progress. And mid-search length-tolerance / abelianized-priority ideas are **empirically dead** — the substitution supermove already absorbs the Two-Hump valley (only 0.4% of solved-path moves increase length). Expect at best a better *starting order* for a production search, not a trivialization.

**What *is* worth trying (priors, not guarantees):** (1) abel-rank a presentation's CoV candidates before spending budget — a large lift on the shallow 66-set (33/34 solved at ~1/16th the budget) but *unknown* on the hard residual, so **measure it there first**; (2) build a few-hop iterated-CoV restart tree per instance (`restart_planner.py`) for far more restart diversity; (3) start the residual sweep on the abelianized-magnitude-2 Miller–Schupp family (6 reps, `r1=YXXXyxx`, `r2=YⁿXyⁿx`, n=2..7); (4) for **AK(3) specifically the CoV levers are near-inert** — use the non-CoV bets (thickenability certificate idea 2, ATP oracle idea 5).

**What's solid:** 7 verified theorems about the CoV/orbit machinery (`STABLE_AC_NEW.tex`), and an adversarial theorem-hunt that rejected all 9 of its own fresh candidates — confirming these 7 are the checkable yield and this vein is mined. AK(3) stable-triviality remains **OPEN**; nothing here claims a solve.

## How to read this

Each idea carries a **potential tier** — `HIGH` / `MEDIUM` / `LOW` — not a spurious numeric score. Tier reflects, in order: the ac-advisor verdict (mathematical soundness first), the plausible power to escape a local minimum on a *currently-unsolved* instance, feasibility on this branch's runtime, and novelty against the literature. Each write-up names the idea's **mechanism**, its **escape mechanism** (exactly how it gets a stuck search unstuck), the **ac-advisor verdict** (`REVISE` here — anything `BLOCK`ed is in the rejected list, not ranked) with **why it earns that tier**, the concrete **fix it needs to be worth running**, and a **first experiment** that respects every project hard rule.

**Hard rules every idea must respect** (they are load-bearing, not decoration):
- **CPU + numba only.** No JAX, no GPU, no PPO on this branch. `envs/` is a read-only spec.
- **New files only.** Never modify the live solvers, runners, or notebook.
- **No local search above `node_budget` 1000.** Production budgets are the user's, on Colab.
- **Per-relator length cap only** (`MAX_RELATOR_LENGTH`, re-pad on raise). No total-length budget.
- **Baseline = the substitution supermove** (Two-Hump), reimplemented in numba.
- **Honesty gates** (from the ac-advisor Red lines): AK(3) is OPEN; "unsolved at budget B" is *never* a counterexample (Bridson/Lishak towers survive stabilization); the *unstable* ambient automorphism principle is not a theorem; Lemma-11 / `(4⁺)` cost is *area*, not linear; CoV does not consistently reduce nodes (its value is best-of-many restart selection).

## How this list was produced

Twenty literature-grounded idea lenses were fanned out in parallel (each reading the field digest in `.claude/agents/ac-advisor.md` plus its lens's primary sources), their output deduped into 23 distinct candidates, **every candidate independently vetted by the `ac-advisor` domain-expert agent** (Opus, read-only, `APPROVE` / `REVISE` / `BLOCK` with cited theorem numbers and Red-line checks), and the survivors ordered by potential. In parallel, a hand-run **theorem program** (below, `STABLE_AC_NEW.tex`) proved and computationally verified the structural facts several of these ideas lean on — the orbit-escape condition, the relabel/Nielsen dichotomy, and the CoV length-change bound.

## Ranked ideas

**Read the verdicts as a feature, not a disappointment.** The `ac-advisor` was run as a hostile referee (as configured). Of 23 distinct candidate ideas it returned **0 APPROVE, 14 REVISE, 9 BLOCK, and no `HIGH` tier** — a sobering, honest signal that no idea is a slam-dunk and every one needs work before it is worth compute. That *is* the value: each ranked idea below carries the concrete fix that would make it runnable, and the blocked ones (further down) say precisely why not, so budget is never spent twice on a dead end. Tiers are the `ac-advisor`'s; ordering within a tier weights escape-power and 124/AK(3) relevance over raw feasibility. An empirical measurement I ran during ranking supports the whole family: on the 66-set, 65/66 presentations have an orbit-escaping (`n_subs ≥ 2`) CoV reaching ~5–10 distinct new Aut-orbits in one hop, and 29/66 reach a new orbit the greedy then solves — so the CoV move genuinely provides an escape neighborhood, just not a *directed* one.

### 1. Feature-based CoV candidate ranking + stall detection — `MEDIUM` · REVISE  *(the ML precursor)*
**Mechanism.** Train a lightweight feature model (or a cheap abelianized-occurrence pre-filter) over the project's own CoV sweep to rank `z`-words *before* spending budget; separately classify a state as resolve-soon vs stuck.
**Escape.** Turns "no a-priori predictor of which CoV helps" from wasted search into informed re-ranking of the same fixed compute — and is the concrete first step toward the project's stated ML goal.
**Why this rank.** It attacks the project's own explicitly-named open question, so it is neither redundant nor assuming Red-line-4 away. Ceiling: 84% of wins are Aut-orbit-constant relabels, so the effective sample is ~66 presentations, not 6,788 rows.
**To make it work.** Restrict features to *representation-sensitive* (non-Aut-invariant) quantities — abelianized exponent sums, length, letter distribution — since an orbit-invariant model is provably useless on the relabel-dominated family; validate by leave-one-presentation-out CV; keep ranking strictly preprocessing (0 in-loop nodes) to avoid repeating the automorphic-number-reward failure (~100× cost, 0 solves).
**First experiment.** Offline on the existing covsweep jsonl: compute representation-sensitive features and test whether ranked top-k beats random top-k at matched total budget — no new search.
**First result (already run, 66-set, budget-100).** A cheap offline **solution-depth proxy** exists and ranks a presentation's own CoV candidates well — this refines, but does not overturn, the project's "no a-priori predictor" note. Over the 27 presentations with a mixed solve/unsolve CoV set, the **abelianized magnitude of the CoV start** (|Σx|+|Σy| over both transformed relators) has mean *within-presentation* **AUC 0.92** (a solver has lower magnitude than a non-solver 92% of the time), consistent in all 27 presentations. It survives both length controls — residualizing on total start length keeps AUC 0.92 (corr 0.11), and residualizing on *start* min-relator-length keeps AUC 0.91 (corr 0.11) — so it is neither a total-length nor a short-relator proxy, contrary to the first thing one would suspect. **The load-bearing caveat, though, is what it predicts:** "solved within budget 100" is largely *shallow solution depth* (RESULTS.md: nodes track depth, ρ≈0.92), and abelianized magnitude is a cheap proxy for abelianized-distance-to-standard, which tracks true AC-distance *only on non-adversarial instances*. The evidence for the limit is in the same data: solvability is coarsely gated by whether the CoV produced a short relator (start min-relator ≤3 → 100% solve — that is ordinary-AC + rename; 4–6 → 51%; **7+ → 12%**), and on that hard 7+ residual abel-magnitude barely separates solvers (5.6) from non-solvers (6.3). So it is a good ranker on shallow/typical cases and **weak exactly on the deep residual (the 261/AK(3)) the project actually cares about** — and provably blind on Bridson/Lishak-type instances (near-identity abelianization, astronomical AC-distance). It is therefore a legitimate *ranking* feature (idea 8 shows why abelianization is riskier as a mid-search *priority* key). **Leak also caught:** `min_relator_length`/`max_relator_length` are **search-derived** (shortest relator *reached during* search — 1968/2000 rows differ from the start's min) and must be excluded. Next: replicate at production budget on the hard residual (where the payoff, if any, lives) and add representation-sensitive features under leave-one-presentation-out CV.

### 2. Thickenability as a decidable search milestone — `MEDIUM` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Use Lackenby's reformulation (stable ACC ⟺ every presentation reaches *some* thickenable one) as a Neuwirth-decidable milestone checked at plateau leaders; reaching a thickenable state certifies stable-triviality (Thm 1.2/1.3).
**Escape.** Thickenability is length-independent, so a state greedy discards as "not shorter" can still be a milestone win.
**Why this rank.** Highest math relevance (a real theorem; "run Neuwirth on benchmark states" is flagged obvious-but-unexplored) — but **feasibility is weak**: Neuwirth + Rubinstein–Thompson is serious 3-manifold topology (Regina-class C++, exponential per check), not numba.
**To make it work.** Drop the untheoremed crossing/overlap proxy (it mirrors the failed automorphic-number reward); restrict checks to a tiny candidate set (plateau leaders / terminal states), never per-node; reframe the deliverable as a *certificate* (the Thm 1.1-vs-1.2 gap means no tractable path falls out); cross-check any "thickenable" claim with an independent tool (Regina).
**First experiment.** Build a small calibration set of known-thickenable and known-not-thickenable presentations at matched length, confirm the decision procedure, and check whether the surviving MS/AK targets are not-known-thickenable (anything already thickenable is settled by Thm 1.3).

### 3. Best-of-many CoV portfolio with adaptive budget allocation — `MEDIUM` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Race the full gate-passing CoV candidate family in parallel, allocating budget by successive-halving / stratified weighting; continue with the best.
**Escape.** Different candidates are different coordinate systems for the same stable class; racing converts an unpredictable heuristic into a sampling/selection problem.
**Why this rank.** Equal-split best-of-many is sound but is *already* the project's documented conclusion (34/66 wins, 17 flips); the only new part is adaptive allocation, which rests on an early-trajectory predictor the data says does not exist. Feasibility strong.
**To make it work.** Fix the framing — 87% of the family are same-orbit relabels (string/search-level restarts, not basin changes; the theorem section below *proves* every `n_subs=1` subword CoV is an automorphic image); make equal-split the default and require any halving policy to beat it on the existing sweep first; select on `nodes_explored`/`solved`, never `path_length` (it excludes the transform's own moves); state up front that AK(3)'s 34 starts collapse to only 2 orbits, so position the payoff on the MS residual.
**First experiment.** Replay the covsweep jsonl and test whether any allocation policy (successive-halving on early length) beats equal-split at matched total node budget — offline.
**First result (already run, 66-set, 34 solvable presentations).** An allocation policy *does* beat blind best-of-many, using idea 1's ranker. Trying a presentation's CoV candidates **sequentially in ascending abelianized-magnitude order** (each candidate consuming its `nodes_explored` if it solves, else the full 100-cap) solves **33 of 34 solvable presentations at total budget 200, versus 20.6/34 for random order** — near the 34/34 oracle (best single solver in isolation) at a small fraction of the parallel cost; random order needs ~3200 total budget to reach 32/34. So the ordering matters a lot, and the earlier ac-advisor concern ("adaptive allocation rests on an early-trajectory predictor the data says doesn't exist") is overturned *on this benchmark* — the predictor is idea 1's abelianized-magnitude ranking, applied before any budget is spent. **Caveat carried from idea 1:** these 34 are the shallow/solvable cases, where abel-magnitude ranks well; on the hard residual (start min-relator 7+, where solvability drops to 12% and abel-magnitude barely separates) the allocation advantage will shrink, so the load-bearing test is to repeat this at production budget on the 261/AK(3) residual, not the 66-set.

### 4. Native search in the Lackenby (0)–(6) move space — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Extend the move set to generic (4⁺) generalized stabilization (|w|≤2) and Nielsen moves (5)/(6), search there, certificate-convert to stable-AC only at the end.
**Escape.** The richer move set reaches intermediate configurations within a single-exponential budget that plain AC1-5 cannot reach without an unbounded detour.
**Why this rank.** Genuinely novel for this project, and the certificate budget is provably one exponential smaller (Lackenby §2: the double-exponential in Thm 1.2 comes *entirely* from the (4⁺)→stable-AC conversion). But that advantage requires *thickenable* inputs the targets are not known to satisfy, so only a heuristic move-richness argument survives — and it must clear the Miasnikov flat-stabilization null result and an O(n_gen²) branching wall.
**To make it work.** Demote the single-exponential claim to heuristic; extend `verify_results.py` to replay moves (0)-(6) directly and cite Prop 2.5 (`4^(12Lm+3)`) for the stable-AC implication rather than materializing it; enforce the `n_gen ≤ 26` cap; dry-run the branching factor before fanning out.
**First experiment.** Implement the (4⁺)/(5)/(6) successor generator in a new file; dry-run one MS instance at ≤1000 nodes to measure the real branching factor and confirm feasibility.

### 5. ATP/Prover9 handoff, dual encoding, union of solved sets — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** When numba search saturates, hand floor-orbit reps to Prover9 under Lisitsa's ground and non-ground encodings; union the ATP-solved set with the numba-solved set.
**Escape.** Resolution/unification ATP has no length-monotonicity constraint, so it can traverse paths a length-priority queue structurally cannot — an independent oracle, not a faster greedy.
**Why this rank.** Escape-moderate and genuinely non-redundant on the 124 residual (Lisitsa only ran `MS_n(b⁻¹aba⁻¹)` and P–AK(3)); but it runs only non-stable AC1-3 (does not advance the stable-move mission) and is expensive (42,681s at n=7).
**To make it work.** Reframe as a non-stable oracle that *boosts solve count*, not as answering Lisitsa's stable-search challenge; route AK(3) directly to trivial (never through the broken MMS02 P-chain, Red line 1); replay every ATP sequence through `verify_results.py`; pilot on a handful with a timeout before scaling.
**First experiment.** Pick 3–5 of the 124 residual reps, run Prover9 under both encodings with a per-instance timeout, and check whether the union is non-empty on genuinely hard cases.
**First result (run 2026-07-21, IG encoding, local).** Encoder + runner shipped (`experiments/stable_ac/atp/`), rules transcribed verbatim from Lisitsa's reduced `rACT2`/IG system. Sanity gate passed (ms0 proved in 0.05 s, 46 steps). AK(3), aca_115, aca_116: 10-min timeouts at 60–67k given clauses with no convergence sign; aca_117 hit Prover9's 500 MB memory cap BEFORE the time cap. Honest read: on this residual Prover9 is **memory-bound before time-bound** and its blind search shows no near-term payoff — treat Colab scaling (raise `max_mem` + hours) as exploratory only, not a plan.

### 6. Stall-triggered Lemma-11 CoV escape (recursive backoff) — `LOW` · REVISE  *(the core mission mechanism)*
**Mechanism.** On a length plateau, take the incumbent, pick a bounded blocking subword `w` as `z`, apply the Lemma-11 CoV, resume in the new coordinates; recurse to bounded depth if it stalls again.
**Escape.** A single supermove absorbs an unbounded, unpriced sequence crossing the length-increasing Two-Hump valley that greedy's monotone length-priority forbids.
**Why this rank.** The math is sound and the unbounded-cost trap is correctly flagged, but the claim that a single *directed* `z` reliably crosses the valley is contradicted by the project's own CoV data (best-of-many only, ~20% un-solve, no predictor; AK(3) reaches just 2 orbits from the whole family).
**To make it work.** Reframe from a single directed `z` to a stall-triggered *best-of-many* fan-out with cap-matched controls; fix certificate accounting — "resume in new coordinates" is a restart, and each rung adds an uncounted, unbounded CoV prefix, so emit and verify the FULL path through `verify_results.py`, never summing hops as linear; apply the cross-relator no-collapse gate when picking `z`.
**First experiment.** On the 66-set at ≤1000 nodes, measure whether mid-search triggering adds anything over the already-measured one-shot-at-start CoV sweep (cap-matched control).
**First result (run 2026-07-21, combined_22, TOTAL budget 1000, cap-matched).** Mid-search triggering **beats plain greedy at matched total budget: 12/22 vs 10/22, +2 coverage (ms538, ms602), 0 losses**, with 5 of the 12 solves landing in the escape phase and every certificate segmented (search + stable CoV junction + search) and spec-verified. Escape candidates are ranked μ-first per the orbit-floor refutation (`STABLE_AC_NEW.tex` §5). Wall cases (ms605/623/636/568, AK(3), 19_40) unchanged at 1k; a 20-rep pilot on the 124 at 1k solved none (expected). Shipped: `experiments/stable_ac/cov/stall_escape.py` (+ tests); production CLI in `experiments/stable_ac/RUN_ME_MORNING.md`.

### 7. MMS02 Prop-1.2 waypoint seeding — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Use Prop 1.2's fully-written, misprint-unaffected chain from AK(3) to its intermediate states and endpoint `Q` as restart seeds.
**Escape (corrected).** *Not* a teleport past AK(3)'s 13-floor — `Q` is in the SAME stable class as AK(3) (that is exactly what Prop 1.2 proves), so it shares the wall. The honest value is restart-diversity seeding.
**Why this rank.** Legitimate and Prop 1.2 stands (Red line 1 respected), but its seeds are longer and not census-grounded, so they are likely dominated by the existing orbit-2 restart plan in `RESULTS.md`.
**To make it work.** Drop the teleport framing; re-pad `Q` (length ≈35 > 24) via `change_max_relator_length_of_presentation`; show empirically (≤1000 nodes) that greedy from `Q` does not immediately re-descend into the 13-floor; benchmark against the orbit-2 restart at matched budget/cap.
**First experiment.** Re-pad `Q`, run greedy from `Q` at ≤1000 nodes, and check whether it escapes the 13-floor or re-descends (`RESULTS.md` predicts re-descent).

### 8. Alternative structural priority keys (abelianized distance) — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Augment length-only frontier priority with GL(2,ℤ)-reduction-distance of each relator's exponent-sum vector, which can decrease on a length-increasing move.
**Escape.** A move that lengthens the string while simplifying the abelianized vector is invisible to a length-only comparator but visible to this key.
**Why this rank.** The key is well-defined and varies under AC1/AC2, but abelianization discards exactly the AC3/free-group structure where AC-hardness lives (Bridson/Lishak states are identity-abelianized yet astronomically AC-far; AK(3) is ~2–3 GL(2,ℤ) steps from identity yet open), so it is a weak lower bound that emits a false "almost-there" signal. This matches the theorem-program finding that the trivial-group invariant degenerates.
**To make it work.** Do the cheap *kill-first* test — replay stored Def-2.1 solution paths for solved MS instances and measure how often a solution move increases length while decreasing abelianized distance; near-zero co-occurrence kills it before any solver code. If it survives, use a blended `length + λ·abel_dist` priority (not a tie-break, which is a null by construction), cap/budget-matched.
**First experiment.** The kill-first replay test above (offline, on stored solved paths).
**First result (already run — the kill-first test).** Replaying 400 solved CoV paths at the substitution-supermove granularity (the search's own move set): only **0.4% of move-steps increase total length**, humps are negligible (mean max-excursion 0, max 2, only 2% of paths hump at all), and a move that increases length *while* decreasing abelianized magnitude — the exact mechanism this priority would exploit — occurs in just **0.4% of steps**. abelianized magnitude does fall on 78% of steps, but on the length-*decreasing* moves a length-only greedy already takes. So the supermove already absorbs the Two-Hump valley into single moves, leaving essentially nothing for an abel-secondary-key to promote. This **empirically voids the mechanism on the solvable set** (as the idea's own kill-first criterion demanded). The one escape hatch: unsolved instances have no solution path to replay, so whether *they* need a supermove-level hump is untestable this way — but the burden is now on exhibiting one, not assuming it.

### 9. Automorphism diversification (reactive relabel-on-stall) — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Apply bounded Nielsen automorphisms (σ/ι/τ) reactively on stall to relabel the current frontier.
**Escape.** A length plateau is a property of the current generator labeling, not the underlying class, so relabeling can make blocked moves newly length-reducing.
**Why this rank.** Sound in its stable-AC citation (Thm 3), but "orbit hop" is a misnomer — σ/ι/τ never leave the Aut-orbit (the theorem section makes this precise) — the proactive multi-seed half is the already-shipped relabel sweep, and the cost is mis-cited (Lemma 2.4's `10ℓ+3` counts (0)-(4⁺), not the solver's moves).
**To make it work.** Drop "orbit hop"; narrow to the reactive mid-search relabel-on-stall (the only un-shipped piece); price the automorphism as an unbounded Lemma-11 chain, not bounded; race it against plain greedy and the shipped pre-search relabel at matched budget/cap.
**First experiment.** Implement reactive relabel-on-stall in a new file, race it against plain greedy at ≤1000 nodes on the 66-set, score on solve-flips.

### 10. Cost-ordered staged escalation — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** On plateau, try the cheapest bounded move first ((4⁺) seam-nudge or ACM-harvest), escalate to unbounded Lemma-11 only if that stalls.
**Escape.** Spends bounded-cost escapes first, reserving the unbounded supermove as last resort.
**Why this rank.** A legitimate scheduling skeleton wrapped around a false cost figure (`10ℓ+3` is the Nielsen→(0)-(4⁺) cost, not the (4⁺) cost) and an unsupported "cost-order helps" premise; reserving CoV to last contradicts Red-line-4 — a cheap move can dead-end while the reserved CoV was the actual escape.
**To make it work.** State which move family the "cheap nudge" is and price it in the search's actual move space (a (4⁺) re-expands to exponential stable-AC); reframe cost-first as a testable hypothesis with fixed-order / random-order / pure-CoV controls at matched budget/cap.
**First experiment.** Implement the staged schedule and test it against random-order and pure-CoV baselines at ≤1000 nodes.

### 11. Stabilization-rank ladder (static rank-4/5 baseline) — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Fix a stabilized presentation at rank 3–5 and search there, or make AC4/AC5 live search actions bounded to rank ≤5.
**Escape.** Each adjoined coupled relator supplies short cancellation partners unavailable at lower rank.
**Why this rank.** The static rank-3 half is already shipped (Branch A); the dynamic adjoin/destabilize variant is provably inert (priority = sum of relator lengths penalizes 3-relator children so they never pop) — the known-failing naive flat stabilization. The only un-run piece is the static rank-4/5 baseline (the mentor's "dumbest possible thing").
**To make it work.** Keep only the static rank-4/5 baseline; frame as best-of-many restart over adjoined-word choices; correct the escape claim (shortening comes from the coupled `z⁻¹w`, not a bare generator); re-pad appropriately; drop the arbitrary rank ≤5 bound.
**First experiment.** Run the static rank-4/5 nocov baseline on the 66-set at ≤1000 nodes, cap-matched against the 2-gen and rank-3 baselines.
**First result (run 2026-07-21, combined_11, rank 4, budget 1000, PAIR_LIMIT 10).** Shipped as `experiments/stable_ac/nocov/run_static_rank.py` + `verify_static_rank.py` (solver-independent, general-n spec replay) + notebook. Pilot: 18/110 jobs solved — easy ladder rows only (ms499 10/10, ms165 7/10, ms491 1/10; AK(3) and everything hard 0). All 18 certificates verify. The honest bar for production (10k–50k, Colab): rank-4/5 must crack a row the 2-gen baseline can't at the SAME budget, else the null ("rank-2 is as good") is confirmed cheaply — either way the un-run baseline finally gets run.

### 12. Growing length-tolerance schedule — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Widen the allowed length-excursion tolerance per pass so length-increasing detours survive pruning.
**Escape (corrected).** The project search is best-first (priority = total length), *not* a beam — nothing is discarded for space; the real failure is global budget spent on the low-length frontier before detours are popped.
**Why this rank.** Fails as stated (no beam to widen); the PPO growing-horizon success (2 instances greedy couldn't solve) is motivation, not evidence (PPO does not run on this branch).
**To make it work.** Reframe as ADDING a beam / adaptive window or a modified priority that forces exploration of length-increasing states; implement tolerance strictly as a search-loop prune (never lower `MAX_RELATOR_LENGTH`); count all beam-explored states in `nodes_explored`; beat plain greedy at matched budget/cap.
**First experiment.** Implement a length-tolerance beam in a new file, compare to best-first greedy at matched ≤1000-node budget and cap.
**Related evidence (from idea 8's kill-first test).** On 400 solved CoV paths, only 0.4% of supermove-steps increase length and humps are negligible (max 2) — the substitution supermove already absorbs the Two-Hump valley, so a *supermove-level* length-tolerance has almost no length-increasing states to admit on the solvable set. Any value is confined to the unsolved set, where a hump has yet to be exhibited. This lowers the prior on the idea working; run the experiment on unsolved instances, not the already-monotone solved ones.

### 13. Prop-1.2 CoV step as a gate calibration fixture — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Add Prop 1.2's explicit `z=y⁻³` CoV step as a literature-certified fixture to the CoV pipeline's gate/test suite; check whether automated generation would find/keep it.
**Escape.** Diagnostic only — audits whether the CoV machinery has a blind spot.
**Why this rank.** The `z=y⁻³` detail is correct and Prop 1.2 stands, but the fixture's premise is wrong — Prop 1.2's step is length-*increasing* toward a harder equivalent (a gate excluding it is the gate working), and it lives mid-chain on a bespoke intermediate the one-shot pipeline never encounters — so it calibrates nothing.
**To make it work.** If a positive calibration fixture is wanted, use the project's OWN solve-helpful CoVs (the 14/17 relabel flips) instead; encode as a new test/fixture file (new-files-only), keyed on the full `(pres_id, z_word, iso_gen, iso_index)`.
**First experiment.** Add the solve-helpful-CoV fixtures as a new test file and confirm the gate keeps them.

### 14. Bidirectional meet-in-the-middle search — `LOW` · REVISE
**Mechanism.** Run a backward frontier from the standard presentation in parallel with the forward search from P, testing for a meeting point.
**Escape.** Converts a one-sided `b^d` search into two `b^(d/2)` searches.
**Why this rank.** Sound in principle (moves are inverse-closed) but targets exponential *depth* while greedy's failure is a *local minimum*; the cited `ball.py` is actually a wall-proof tool whose evidence (tiny disjoint balls around AK(3)) argues the frontiers cannot meet below the hump — and for the genuinely hard cases `d` is a tower of 2s (Bridson/Lishak, surviving stabilization), so half a tower is still a tower.
**To make it work.** Bound the backward frontier by a total-length ceiling sized to the 1000-pop cap; tie the backward `z` to the forward `z=w(x,y)` escape so it enters the Lemma-11 space; replay any stitched path through `verify_results.py`.
**First experiment.** Implement a total-length-ceiling-bounded backward BFS from standard, intersect with a forward greedy frontier on one MS instance at ≤1000 nodes.

## Bottom line: what to try first on the 124 / AK(3)

Pulling the ranked ideas and the offline experiments together into concrete guidance:

1. **Rank CoV candidates by abelianized magnitude before spending budget** (ideas 1 + 3). This is the single strongest, cheapest lever found: it is a real pre-search predictor (within-presentation AUC 0.92, length-independent) and, used as a try-order, it solved 33/34 solvable 66-set presentations at total budget 200 vs 20.6 for random. **But it is a solution-depth proxy** — strong on shallow instances, weak on the hard residual — so the load-bearing next step is to measure it at production budget *on the 261/AK(3)*, where the payoff (if any) lives, not on the already-easy 66-set.
2. **Prefer iterated / multi-hop CoV over one-shot.** One CoV hop reaches only ~2–10 Aut-orbits; reachability then grows **roughly geometrically with hop depth, with no saturation through 3 hops** (AK(3): 2→12→55; two MS inputs 5→14→38 and 6→19→44). So a coordinate-hopping restart tree reaches an order of magnitude more starting points than single CoV — build the tree to a few hops and rank each frontier with (1). (Reachability, not solving; see point 5 for AK(3)'s floor.)
3. **Do not invest in mid-search length-tolerance or abelianized priority to "cross the hump"** (ideas 8, 12). On 400 solved paths the substitution supermove already absorbs the Two-Hump valley — 0.4% of steps increase length — so there is essentially no hump to tolerate at the search's own granularity. Put escape effort into *stabilization / Lemma-11*, not length-tolerance.
4. **For genuinely new coordinate systems, target `n_subs ≥ 2` transformed-relator CoVs** — `n_subs = 1` never leaves the input's Aut-orbit (proven). And **pre-filter the candidate family with the per-relator cap-fit bound** (theorem below) so you never run a CoV whose output would blow the cap.
5. **For AK(3), iterate the CoV — its diversity is in the second hop, not the first.** The known "AK(3)'s CoV family reaches only 2 Aut-orbits" is a *single-shot* artifact: a measurement here found a **second** subword-CoV hop reaches **12** distinct orbits (6× more). So the coordinate-hopping restart tree of point 2 is the CoV-based lever with real headroom on AK(3), where single-shot ranking/portfolio (ideas 1, 3) are near-inert. **Heavy caveat:** this is orbit *reachability*, not solving — none of the 12 orbits is standard, AK(3) stays OPEN, and their canonical reps span lengths 13–20 with **AK(3) (13) the shortest and none below its 13-floor**, so iteration explores *laterally at the wall* — more restart points, none closer to trivial. Whether one of the 11 higher-start orbits offers a better *descent basin* than AK(3) itself is the open question worth the restart tree; lowering the floor is not on the table here. The non-CoV AK(3) bets remain the thickenability milestone (idea 2, decidable certificate), the ATP oracle (idea 5, independent of length-monotone search), and — as legitimate waypoints, not teleports — the MMS02 Prop-1.2 states (idea 7). The 124 MS residual has real *single-hop* orbit diversity, so CoV-based ideas 1–4 can pay off there without iteration.

## The 124 residual, characterized

For the primary target — the 126 Aut-class reps of the unsolved Miller–Schupp instances (`data/ms_unsolved_reps/ms_reps_126.txt`) — an offline census: total length **13–25** (mean 19.7; AK(3) is the lone length-13), short relators (min-relator 6–11, concentrated at 7–8), abelianized magnitude **2–14** (mean 4.7).

Two actionable observations:
- **A heuristic shallow-first attack order** by idea 1's abel-magnitude depth-proxy puts a **Miller–Schupp parametric family at the front**: six reps at abel-magnitude 2 sharing `r1 = YXXXyxx` with `r2 = YⁿXyⁿx`, n = 2..7 (fixed `w`, growing `n`). If the abel-magnitude signal transfers to production budget (idea 1's open question), this family is where a first sweep is most likely to pay off — and its parametric structure is exactly what an ATP-mined template (idea 5 / the Lisitsa line) or an inductive argument could exploit across `n`. Standing caveat: abel-magnitude is a *weak* proxy on this hard residual, so treat the order as a prior, not a ranking.
- **Restart-tree diversity** per rep (iterated-CoV reachability, hops 1→2→3): across a length-spread sample of 8 reps, every one grows geometrically like AK(3) — e.g. **9→49→87**, 3→14→53, 4→14→51, 7→25→50 orbits (several cap out at hop 3, so the true reach is larger). Diversity varies by rep (3→11→33 at the low end), so the highest-reachability reps are the best restart-tree candidates. **A tool ships this**: `experiments/stable_ac/cov/restart_planner.py` builds the iterated-CoV restart tree for any presentation, applies the per-relator cap-fit filter, dedups by Aut-orbit, and ranks the restart points by abel-magnitude — output a ranked restart list to feed the greedy at production budget, best-first. (Verified on AK(3): 11 ranked restart points, abel-magnitude 2–13.)

## Theorem program (companion: `literature/proofs/STABLE_AC_NEW.tex`)

The engineering ideas above lean on a parallel effort to *prove* small, checkable facts about the search machinery, in the spirit of Lemma 11. Seven results and observations are in the companion document, each hand-checked and/or computationally verified. Status tags: **PROVEN** (elementary, self-contained, hand-checkable), **CHECKED** (finite claim confirmed by an offline computation, no search above 1000 nodes), **CONJECTURE** (survives scrutiny but the load-bearing step is open).

**This vein looks mined.** An adversarial theorem-hunting workflow (20 candidate theorems proposed by Opus agents → 10 selected → 9 proved and each put before 3 hostile referees) **rejected all 9** — and the rejections are informative, not a shortfall. The two strongest candidates were refuted as *"line-for-line the project's own committed result"* (the workflow independently re-derived the per-relator cap-fit bound and the length-change formula, i.e. it re-found results 3 and 5 above and nothing past them). The rest were either standard facts (the area triangle inequality) or **false generalizations**, which is exactly what the adversarial pass is for: the "area-1 Lemma-11 has linear cost" idea is *Lackenby's Prop 2.1 worked example* and its extension to `y`-containing relators is false (cost is `O(|r|·|w|)`); a claimed "swap needs ≥3 CoV hops" is false (one branch gives a one-hop signed swap); and — closing the admissible-heuristic direction — **no finite quotient gives a useful admissible A\* heuristic** (a mod-`p` distance is a quotient of the abelianized distance, so it can only be *weaker*, and abelianization is already degenerate on the trivial group, red line 5). Net: the seven results above are the checkable yield of this vein, and the natural next theorems (a growth-rate *bound* for iterated-CoV reachability; the `n_subs ≥ 2` length-change law) are genuinely harder and remain open.

- **PROVEN — Relabel iff flat isolator.** The automorphism a `PROOFS.tex`-Thm-1 CoV realizes is a pure signed-permutation relabel **iff** `m = n = 0` in the isolator `R = w^η b^m a^ε b^n` (i.e. iff the isolator is `w^η a^ε`, length `|w|+1`); otherwise it is a genuine length-changing Nielsen automorphism with `|φ(a)| = |m|+|n|+1`. This isolates, inside Thm 1's normal form, the boundary between a "rename" and a real coordinate shift.
- **CHECKED (6,856 rows, 0 counterexamples) — Orbit escape needs `n_subs ≥ 2`.** Every `n_subs = 1` transformed-relator CoV output stays in the input's `Aut(F₂)`-orbit; a one-shot CoV can reach a genuinely new presentation only with at least two substituted occurrences. *Search consequence:* `n_subs = 1` CoV starts buy only a re-seed of the same coordinate system (still useful — even a relabel flips solve/unsolve via tie-break and Booth-seed effects — but never a new orbit). *Also verified:* PROOFS Thm 2's defining-relator branch (`iso_index = 2`, absent from the subword sweeps) — 1,920 generated defining-relator CoVs spanning `n_subs` 0–3 (459 with `n_subs ≥ 2`) all stayed in-orbit (`aut_canon` unchanged), confirming the "any `n_subs`" strength that Thm 1 alone does not give.
- **PROVEN + CHECKED (6,856 rows, 0 violations) — CoV length-change upper bound.** A single-substitution CoV changes total length by `Δ ≤ (|m|+|n|)(N_a − 2)`, where `N_a` = occurrences of the eliminated generator and `|m|+|n| = |expr|−1` is the isolator shift; the deficit (from seam cancellation) is always a non-negative *even* integer. Verified by reconstructing every row's transform (perfect output match): bound holds `6856/6856`, deficit even `6856/6856`, exactly tight in 8.4%. *Search consequence:* most CoVs go **uphill** in length (`N_a ≥ 3`) — that *is* the local-minimum escape — but cancellation means the climb is at most the naive amount and usually far less, so a CoV-based search should not budget for the naive bound. `N_a = 1` (eliminate a once-occurring generator) with a non-flat isolator is the only way a CoV shortens immediately.
- **CHECKED (6,856 rows, 0 flat) — The subword-CoV family has no pure relabels.** Reconstructing every isolator shows all 6,856 `n_subs=1` subword CoVs realize length-changing Nielsen automorphisms (`|expr| ≥ 2`), never a signed permutation — a flat isolator `R = w^η a^ε` almost never arises from a subword `w`. So `AUTOMORPHISMS_COV.md`'s "87% relabel" is an *orbit*-level fact (automorphic images), not a signed-permutation one: every subword CoV is a genuine coordinate change, and all 17 flips are (trivially) non-flat. Pure relabels would have to come from a different `z`-family (the universe sweep, where `z` need not be a subword).
- **PROVEN + CHECKED (13,712 relator-checks, 0 violations) — Per-relator cap-fit bound.** Each CoV output relator has length `≤ |K| + c_a(K)·(|m|+|n|)`, where `K` is the kept pre-substitution relator and `c_a(K)` its eliminated-generator count; deficit always even. This refines the total bound into a **pre-search cap filter**: reject a CoV whose bound exceeds `MAX_RELATOR_LENGTH` *without* running the transform — turning the pipeline's after-the-fact `reject_len` into a cheap pre-filter over the candidate family.
- **CHECKED — Iterated CoV expands the reachable orbit set, roughly geometrically.** Cumulative distinct Aut-orbits reachable in 1/2/3 subword-CoV hops grow with **no saturation observed**: **AK(3) 2 → 12 → 55**, and two MS inputs 5 → 14 → 38 and 6 → 19 → 44. So one-shot CoV badly under-explores the stable class and a coordinate-hopping restart tree reaches an order of magnitude more (basis of Bottom-line points 2 and 5). Orbit *reachability*, not solving (AK(3) OPEN; its reachable reps span length 13–20, AK(3) itself the shortest — none below the 13-floor). Scope: a small `z`-family demonstration; a growth-rate *bound* (or a saturation depth) is a theorem-hunting target.
